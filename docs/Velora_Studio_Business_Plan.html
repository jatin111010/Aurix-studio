Confidential — Business Plan

Velora Studio

AI Studio & Sales Engine for Indian Merchants on WhatsApp

Revised strategy · Inbound acquisition · Subscription model
Version 2.0 · July 2026

1. Executive Summary

Velora Studio is a B2B SaaS product built for Indian e-commerce sellers, home businesses, and local retail brands. The entire customer experience lives inside WhatsApp — no website onboarding, no design tools, no technical skills required.

Merchants upload a raw product photo, choose simple options via buttons (background, vibe, studio vs ad), and receive polished marketing-ready images in seconds. After 2 free studio shots, they subscribe via Razorpay to unlock monthly credit balances.

Unfair advantage: Unlike passive SaaS that waits for signups, Velora is built as an inbound conversion engine — Meta/Instagram ads and referrals drive opted-in merchants to WhatsApp, where value is proven in under 60 seconds and subscriptions close in the same chat thread.

Strategic change from v1: Google Maps lead scraping and automated cold WhatsApp outreach are removed. Acquisition is 100% opt-in (ads, content, referrals, partnerships) for compliance, trust, and sustainable unit economics.

2. Revised Workflow (5 Stages)

Stage Name What happens
1 Acquire (opt-in) Meta/Instagram ads, Reels, referrals, and partner channels drive merchants to Click-to-Chat on WhatsApp. User explicitly opts in.
2 Activate (60-second wow) Welcome message → upload product photo → 3 background buttons → first studio result delivered in chat. No long interview on message one.
3 Guide (conversational) OpenAI manages follow-up choices: vibe, studio vs social ad, optional headlines. Button-based — no text prompts required.
4 Generate (Photoroom) Raw image + choices routed to Photoroom Plus API: background removal, AI scene, relighting. Ad mode adds copy + template layout (premium credit).
5 Convert & retain After 2 free images, paywall + Razorpay payment link. Brand memory, festival packs, and referral credits drive retention and expansion.

3. Product Strategy

3.1 Core experience principles

  • WhatsApp-first: Upload, choose, receive — all in chat.
  • Buttons over prompts: Marble / Wood / Studio / Sunlight — no AI literacy needed.
  • Fast proof: First result before any lengthy onboarding.
  • Honest marketing: Promise “studio-quality product shots,” not unverified “4K” unless output is guaranteed.

3.2 Studio vs Social Media Ad fork

After the first image, the bot asks:

“Do you need a Normal Studio Photoshoot or a Social Media Ad Post?”

Mode Output Credit cost
Studio Clean product on chosen background with professional lighting 1 studio credit
Ad post Product + AI headline/sticker (e.g. “Festival Sale — 15% OFF”) on styled template 2 ad credits (higher API + OpenAI cost)

3.3 Free tier

  • 2 free studio images per phone number (lifetime or rolling — configurable).
  • Ad mode available only after subscription or optional top-up pack.
  • Quota balance visible in WhatsApp: “Balance: 38 studio · 5 ad”.

3.4 Brand memory (retention)

After 2nd generation, store lightweight preferences in database:

  • Product category (jewellery, food, clothing, etc.)
  • Preferred background and language (Hindi / English)
  • Last used vibe and ad headline style

Enables faster repeat flows and festival campaign nudges (Diwali, Rakhi, wedding season).

3.5 Optional add-ons (post-MVP)

  • ₹199 one-time pack: 5 studio shots for hesitant non-subscribers.
  • Annual billing: ~2 months free (e.g. Growth at ₹17,999/year) for cash flow.
  • Agency tier: Multi-brand / white-label for listing agencies (+₹1,500/mo add-on).
  • Web dashboard: OTP login, quota history, asset download — after 100+ paying users.

4. Go-to-Market (No Cold Scraping)

4.1 Primary acquisition channels

Channel Tactic Why it works
Meta / Instagram ads “Try 2 free studio shots on WhatsApp” + Click-to-Chat High intent, Meta-compliant opt-in, measurable CPA
Reels / Shorts Before/after product transformations + bio link Low-cost demos; visual product sells itself
Referrals Invite a shop → both get +3 studio credits Low CAC; strong in local merchant networks
Partnerships Photographers, packaging vendors, Shopify/Woo agencies Trusted distribution to ideal customer profile
Marketplace sellers Content targeting Meesho/Amazon listing quality pain Clear ROI story: better photos → more sales

4.2 What we deliberately avoid

  • Google Maps scraping and unsourced phone lists
  • Automated cold WhatsApp blasts to non-opted-in numbers
  • Unlimited subscription tiers (margin risk)
  • Heavy OpenAI “interview” before first image (kills activation)

4.3 Compliance

  • Opt-in only; store consent timestamp per user
  • Easy STOP / unsubscribe in every flow
  • Meta-approved templates for any proactive messaging outside 24h window
  • Respect TRAI / DND norms — no purchased lead lists

5. Financial Architecture & Subscription Tiers

Monthly subscription model with separate studio and ad credit pools. Margins benefit from industry-standard usage breakage (not all subscribers exhaust caps).

5.1 Recommended plans

Plan Target market Price (INR/mo) Studio credits Ad credits
Starter Home bakers, boutique shops ₹999 40 5
Growth D2C brands, Instagram sellers ₹1,799 80 12
Pro E-commerce shops, small agencies ₹2,999 150 25

5.2 Unit economics (conservative)

Cost component Estimate per asset
Studio image (Photoroom + light OpenAI) ₹6 – ₹9
Ad post (Photoroom + copy + layout) ₹10 – ₹14

Starter plan — full usage worst case:
40 × ₹8 + 5 × ₹12 ≈ ₹380 API cost on ₹999 revenue → ~62% gross margin.
Real-world margin is higher due to unused credits and partial ad usage.

5.3 Margin protection rules

  • Ad mode costs 2× internally vs studio (reflected in separate ad credit pool).
  • No unlimited plans at launch.
  • Three tiers only — simple to sell and support.
  • Track funnel: ad click → first photo → second photo → payment (for CPA optimization).

7. Key Metrics to Track

  • Acquisition: Cost per WhatsApp conversation (from Meta ads)
  • Activation: % who upload first photo within 5 minutes
  • Conversion: % who use both free images
  • Revenue: Free → paid conversion rate; ARPU by plan
  • Retention: Month-2 active rate; credits used vs granted (breakage)
  • Quality: Regeneration rate; support tickets per 100 users

8. Summary — What Changed from Original Plan

Removed

Added

  • Funnel analytics from day one
